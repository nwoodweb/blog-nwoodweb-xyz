Use of Euler's Method in Numerical Analysis

What is Euler's Method?

The vast majority of differential equations cannot be solved by guessing or with integrative techniques in an elementary fashion. We must develop a means to approximating these solutions.

This method of numerical approximation was developed by Leonhardt Euler, a Swiss mathemetician

This method involves the development of a polygonal curve that roughly resembles the differential equation that we wish to approximate. This is accomplished as shown below

$$ y' = f(x,y) \ with \ y(x) = y_{i} $$

$$ y_{i+1} = y_i + h*f(x_i,y_i) $$

$$ x = x_i + h $$

Example

Given the following information:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = xy , \ y(0) = 1, \ h = .1 $$ for two steps

$$ y_{.1} = 1 + .1(0) = 1 $$

$$ y_{.2} = 1 + .1((.1)(1)) = 1.01 $$

y(2) is approximately 1.01
